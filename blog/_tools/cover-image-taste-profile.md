# Blog Cover Image Taste Profile

Use this note before generating or replacing blog cover images for Harshal Dasani's blog.

## Core Taste

Harshal prefers cover images that feel like premium editorial storytelling, not flat infographics.

The image should have one strong metaphor that is instantly readable on mobile. It can be clever, even slightly satirical, but it should still feel polished, serious, and business-magazine quality.

Good direction:
- Cinematic animated editorial illustration.
- A clear story or visual metaphor.
- Premium finance/media feel.
- Mobile-first readability.
- Bold subjects, simple composition, minimal tiny labels.
- High contrast, strong depth, rich lighting.
- Navy, graphite, gold, electric blue, clean white highlights.
- Intelligent satire, not childish comedy.

Avoid:
- Dense data slides.
- "What's in / what's out" table-style covers.
- Tiny unreadable text.
- Random loose stock-photo visuals.
- Childish cartoon style.
- Meme-style exaggeration.
- Cluttered charts or dashboard screenshots.
- Real logos or official brand marks.
- Generated images placed loose in the Features root.

## Preferred Process

1. First propose 2 creative concepts, not final images.
2. Let Harshal choose the direction.
3. Write a detailed generation prompt for approval.
4. Generate only after approval.
5. Inspect the image for readability, text quality, mobile crop, and overall taste.
6. Save the final asset only inside the relevant blog post folder.
7. Prefer a WebP for page rendering and keep a PNG for metadata/social previews.
8. Update the blog post, blog listing, RSS/feed, Open Graph, Twitter image, and JSON-LD image references.

## Reference Direction That Worked

For the Indian IT article, the approved taste was:

"AI Express has already left the station."

Scene: an old but polished railway station platform at twilight, with a futuristic AI train leaving toward a high-tech city. Indian IT representatives stand on the platform with luggage labelled TCS, Infosys, and Wipro, waving as the train departs. The metaphor is clear: Indian IT is late to the AI transition.

Why it worked:
- It was story-led, not data-led.
- It looked premium and cinematic.
- It was readable on mobile.
- It used a market/business metaphor without becoming childish.
- It replaced a dense informational cover with a memorable editorial image.

## Prompt Style

Prompts should be production-grade and specific:
- Asset type and use case.
- Scene and metaphor.
- Main subject and supporting details.
- Composition and mobile readability.
- Visual style and palette.
- Mood.
- Exact text allowed in the image.
- Strong negative prompt.

Always keep text in the image limited to a few essential labels. The visual metaphor should do the work.
